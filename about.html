Cool Spots Graz: About this dataset

← Back to the map View on GitHub

A unified, ready-to-use point-of-interest dataset of places in Graz where people can cool down on hot summer days: drinking fountains, public libraries, churches (cool, shaded interiors), and parks/green spaces. Including a flag for the City of Graz's official “Coole Räume” (cool-rooms) program.

Motivation

During Graz's increasingly frequent heatwaves, the City of Graz runs a public „Orte der Abkühlung“ / „Coole Räume“ campaign that points residents to 10 inner-city churches and 3 climate-controlled library branches as official cooling spots. However, this campaign list is not published as structured open data, and it ignores other relevant, freely available cooling infrastructure such as the city's ~180 public drinking fountains and ~90 parks and green spaces. This dataset conflates four open data sources into one table with a consistent schema and a “Coole Räume” flag, so it can be used directly to build a “where can I cool down in Graz” map, app, or accessibility analysis.

“It's 35°C in Graz. Where can I cool down, and which spots are part of the city's official cooling program?”

The “Coole Räume” flag was derived by cross-referencing the official list published by the City of Graz: graz.net/orte-der-abkuehlung-33368 (article dated 5 June 2025). Data sources were last checked and verified between early June and 2026-06-12, reflecting their state as of mid-June 2026. Individual sources update on varying schedules, so the dataset represents a snapshot of availability and current operational status.

Collection & processing method

  • Each Stadt Graz OGD layer (fountains, libraries, churches) was queried via the ArcGIS REST query endpoint.
  • Park/green-space data was queried from OpenStreetMap via the Overpass API for the Graz city area, filtered to named leisure/landuse features.
  • Water playgrounds and spray mist installations were identified through Stadt Graz public announcements and local databases.
  • All coordinates were validated and standardized to WGS84 (EPSG:4326).
  • The final dataset was merged, deduplicated, and enriched with the official “Coole Räume” flag.

Map layers & color coding

The interactive map is organized by cooling spot type (use layer control on the right to filter): fountains, libraries, churches, parks, water playgrounds, and spray cooling installations. Red markers indicate official “Coole Räume” cooling spots designated by the City of Graz. All other colors show the spot type.

Data filtering & scope decisions

Libraries: We include all 8 Stadtbibliothek Graz branches (public city library), excluding university libraries and specialized collections. All Stadtbibliothek locations offer free building access with no membership required to cool off; the membership fee (~€20/year) applies only to borrowing materials. Three of these (Zanklhof, West-Eggenberg, Nord-Geidorf) are designated as official “Coole Räume” and appear with red markers.

Churches: All 10 churches in this dataset are designated as official “Coole Räume” by the City of Graz and appear with red markers. We include only officially designated churches (not all churches in the OGD dataset), as not all are publicly accessible or suitable as cooling spots. These churches offer open, cool interiors with seating during their regular opening hours.

Water playgrounds (Wasserspielplätze): We include 3 public water playgrounds in Graz that offer free, spontaneous cooling through splash zones, water features, and fountains. These are ideal for families and provide a more active cooling option than passive spots. All are operated by the City of Graz and have no entry fees.

Public swimming pools (Schwimmbäder): We include 6 public swimming and bathing facilities operated by Grazer Freizeitbetriebe: Augartenbad, Stukitzbad, Bad Eggenberg, Bad Straßgang, Ragnitzbad, and Bad zur Sonne. While entry fees apply (unlike free cooling options), these facilities offer the most effective cooling during extreme heat and are important infrastructure for public health during heatwaves. Facilities include both seasonal outdoor pools (Freibäder) and year-round indoor pools (Hallenbäder).

Spray mist cooling (Sprühnebel-Lanzen): We include 2 test installations of spray mist cooling systems operated by the City of Graz (Holding Graz) as of June 2026. These innovative systems provide relief on extremely hot days through fine water mist. This is an emerging cooling technology in Graz, inspired by Vienna's “Sommerspritzer” program. Availability and permanent deployment may change; check with the city for current status.

Drinking fountains: The dataset includes all 183 fountains from Stadt Graz OGD as of June 2026. However, some fountains may be out of order (außer Betrieb) at any given time. For current operational status, check the Holding Graz fountain directory.

Public water access / bathing: Graz has no officially designated free public swimming spots or beaches within city limits. While the Mur river flows through Graz, it is not typically used for public bathing. This makes the other cooling infrastructur: fountains, parks, libraries, churches, and water playgrounds, even more valuable during heatwaves.

Update strategy

The Stadt Graz OGD layers and OpenStreetMap are both updated on an ongoing basis. To refresh this dataset, re-run merge_graz.py after re-downloading the raw source files from the endpoints listed above, and re-check the “Coole Räume” list at graz.net/orte-der-abkuehlung-33368 for changes.
